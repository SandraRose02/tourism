TRIVANDRUM

Trivandrum, the capital city of Kerala, India, is a vibrant blend of rich heritage, cultural diversity, and natural beauty. Situated along the southwest coast of the Arabian Sea, Trivandrum offers visitors a captivating mix of historical landmarks, pristine beaches, and lush greenery.

KOVALAM BEACH

One of Trivandrum's most iconic attractions, the Napier Museum is a treasure trove of historical artifacts, ancient sculptures, and traditional Kerala art forms. Adjacent to the museum lies the Thiruvananthapuram Zoo, home to a diverse array of wildlife species, including elephants, tigers, and exotic birds. Visitors can explore the museum's impressive collection and stroll through the zoo's lush gardens, making it an ideal destination for families and history enthusiasts alike.

PADMANABHASWAMY TEMPLE

A symbol of Trivandrum's spiritual heritage and architectural grandeur, the Padmanabhaswamy Temple is an ancient Hindu shrine dedicated to Lord Vishnu. This magnificent temple, known for its Dravidian-style architecture and intricate stone carvings, is a marvel to behold. Visitors can explore its sacred halls, marvel at the towering gopuram (temple tower), and witness the elaborate rituals and ceremonies conducted by the temple priests.
